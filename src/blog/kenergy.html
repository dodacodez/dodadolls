But boy, Ryan's acting talent makes all up for it! ### Ryan Gosling's Ken is a Revolutionary Role When the first images of Ryan Gosling as Ken in the upcoming Barbie movie were released, not only me, but also the internet was divided. Some people were excited to see the actor take on such a iconic role, while others were less than impressed. However, there's no denying that Gosling's take on Ken is revolutionary. For decades, Ken has been portrayed as the perfect but dull male doll. An accessory to Barbie, an empty shell of a man. He's always been handsome and athletic, but his clothing choices were few. Gosling's Ken promises to be different. He's not afraid to show his flaws, he is self-reflecting and even a little bit goofy.
This is a refreshing change of pace for the character of Ken. It's also a sign of the times. In recent years, there's been a growing movement to challenge traditional gender stereotypes. And Gosling's Ken is a perfect example of this. He's not afraid to be himself, even if that means being different from the traditional Ken. ### Gosling's Ken is a sign of progress This Ken is more than just a refreshing change of pace for the character, Gosling even has coined his own term: "Kenergy". He is devoted to Barbie and yet does not want to go under in the wave of all the other "Kens". The borders get blurred: Ryan is so into being Ken that sometimes it is hard to tell where Ryan ends and his Ken role begins. "Kenergy" is a term that has been used to describe the energy or vibe that Ken is giving off in all the promo images for the movie. It's a combination of confidence, sass, and a little bit of camp. Some people have compared Ken's kenergy to that of a '90s boy band member, while others have said that he looks like he's about to drop a fire album. The Ken memes that have flooded the internet since the images were released are a testament to the power of kenergy. People have been using the memes to express their own feelings about Ken, as well as to make jokes about the character. Some of the most popular memes include Ken posing with a variety of objects, such as a banana, a surfboard, and a Barbie Dreamhouse. Others show Ken in different scenarios, such as attending a pool party or going on a date. ### Gosling's natural comedic timing - Kencurious, Kenfused, Kenergy Gosling knows how to deliver a line in a way that is both funny, believable and relatable. This is evident in many of his previous roles, such as in "Crazy, Stupid, Love", as well as various interviews (such as on the Graham Norton show, where he delivered comedy gold whilst pairing with Harrison Ford).
Second, Gosling is not afraid to make himself look ridiculous. He is willing to embrace the campiness of the role and to play it up for laughs. This is evident in the first images of Gosling as Ken, where he is seen wearing crazy outfits and posing in a variety of ridiculous ways. Third, Gosling is surrounded by a talented cast that is also willing to play up the humour of the role. This includes grand comedians like Will Ferrell (playing the CEO of Mattel) and Kate McKinnon (playing the Barbie oracle). In one image, Ken is seen wearing a pink tank top and white briefs. He is posing in a way that is meant to be sexy, but Gosling's expression makes it clear that he is not taking himself too seriously. ### He's "just Ken" In a recent music video released by Warner Bros., Ken is singing and dancing to the song called "I'm just Ken." The composition is actually really good, catchy and funny, and Gosling's performance is hilarious. The song "I'm Just Ken" is a satirical take on the traditional Ken character. It is sung from Ken's perspective, and it expresses his feelings of insecurity and inadequacy. Ken sings about how he always feels like he is second best to Barbie, and how he is constantly trying to live up to her expectations. The song is both funny and poignant, and it provides a glimpse into the inner workings of Ken's mind.
